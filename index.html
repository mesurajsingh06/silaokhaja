Born in Antiquity

The Culinary Jewel of Ancient Nalanda

Legend holds that when Lord Buddha traveled through the ancient city of Nalanda, he was offered this highly intricate multi-layered sweet by local residents. Amazed by its delicate crisp structure, it quickly became a revered staple of the kingdom.

Centuries later, the legendary Sah community of master halwais painstakingly passed the secret method from generation to generation, keeping the strict mathematical art of the 52 layers purely intact.

Today, this distinct recipe is officially recognized globally via the GI Tag (Geographical Indication No. 611), certifying that authentic premium Khaja can only ever be created within the unique soil and microclimate of Silao.

The Master Craft

The Ritual of 52 Layers

Our production process values time, patience, and meticulous physical skill above modern fast machinery.

01

Preparing Dough

Premium wheat flour is rhythmically kneaded with strictly measured indigenous mineral-rich spring water.

02

Rolling Sheets

Artisans meticulously roll the dough into continuous, beautifully transparent, micro-thin sheets.

03

Layering Pastry

The sheets are perfectly stacked exactly 52 times, brushed delicately with pure ghee between each fold.

04

Hand Frying

The pastries are carefully lowered into small-batch shallow kadhais, expanding beautifully into individual layers.

05

Luxury Packing

Cooled slowly, then instantly arranged into airtight premium boxes to maintain perfect texture shield.

Frequently Asked Questions

Authentic Silao Khaja features a strict mathematical distribution of 52 micro-thin individual layers. The local art relies deeply on the distinct natural mineral content of the local groundwater table in Silao, Bihar, creating an unparalleled light crispness that cannot be duplicated anywhere else globally.

Due to the pure dry baking and specialized frying technique that thoroughly removes residual moisture, our Classic and Ghee Khajas comfortably last fresh up to 21-30 days stored inside a standard dry airtight container without requiring synthetic chemical stabilizers.

The Geographical Indication (GI Tag No. 611) legal status explicitly prevents generic industrial bakeries outside the designated geographic zone from mimicking the name, guaranteeing your box comes directly from verified multi-generational family guilds in Nalanda.
